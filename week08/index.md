# Entanglement and Quantum Communication

```{note} Learning objectives (Week 8)
1. Explain how a $\chi^{(2)}$ crystal converts one pump photon into a pair of daughter
   photons (SPDC), and why the process is parametric amplification of vacuum
   fluctuations --- the same squeeze operator as in Week 7.
2. Compute what a beam splitter does to quantum states in the operator language, derive
   the Hong--Ou--Mandel dip, and relate its depth to photon indistinguishability.
3. Derive Bell's 1964 inequality and its experiment-ready CHSH form, prove the
   local-realism bound $|S|\le 2$, and explain the quantum maximum $2\sqrt{2}$
   (Tsirelson).
4. Prove the no-cloning theorem and apply it to quantum communication: BB84 and the
   $25\%$ intercept--resend error rate, CV-QKD with coherent states, and quantum
   teleportation.
```

**Reading:** Gerry & Knight, *Introductory Quantum Optics*, Ch. 6; the 2022 Nobel

**Opening puzzle.** Einstein asked, in effect: does the Moon exist when nobody looks?
For thirty years this sounded like philosophy --- a matter of taste about what "exists"
between measurements. Then, in 1964, John Bell turned it into an inequality: a
*measurable* bound that every local, realistic worldview must obey, and quantum
mechanics predicts is violated. This week covers the deepest argument in the history of
physics, how it was settled in the laboratory (the 2022 Nobel Prize), and how the same
entangled photons became a technology: keys that cannot be copied, and states that can
be teleported.
